preparing for a cat

feeding

foods to avoid

Alcohol, avocado, chocolate, coffee, caffeine, citrus, coconut, grapes, milk/diary, nuts, onions, garlic, & chives.

tip

Many people feed baby food to their cat or kitten when they are refusing to eat or not eating well. Before feeding them any baby food, be sure to check the ingredients for onions or garlic.

information taken from: ASPCA 2017, General Cat Care

grooming

bathing

Bathing is only necessary if your cat becomes very dirty or gets into something sticky or smelly.

brushing

You should brush your cat once or twice a week to maintain a nice, clean coat.

skin problems

Skin problems are marked by excessive scratching, chewing, or licking. If you notice these symptoms, you should seek veterinary care.

shedding

Your cat's fur will regularly shed. For easy maintenance, regularly groom your cat and vacuum the hair.

information taken from: ASPCA 2017, Cat Grooming Tips

litterbox

how to litter train

Litter training your new cat may seem like a daunting task, hence it not being fully detailed here. For a more thorough guide, please check out the Animal Humane Society.

tips

For every cat-accessible floor of the home, there shoud be atleast one litterbox. Each litterbox should be placed in a quiet, easily-accessible location. You should scoop waste at least once a day.

information taken from: the Humane Society 2017, Litter Box 101: Preventing and Solving Litter Box Problems

adopting a cat

adoption tips

considering adoption

Before adopting a cat, make sure that everyone in the home has consented and is prepared. Ensure that the responsibilities have been settled between involved parties and that the supplies are on hand. Lastly, ensure your cat is spayed or neutered!

preparing the home

Before you introduce your cat to its new home, make sure there is a cozy place for them in every room. Remove any items that run chances of being soiled on or broken. Provide toys and other safe distractions, alongside removing any plants that could be poisonous
